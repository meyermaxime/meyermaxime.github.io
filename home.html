Hi!

I am Maxime Meyer, a PhD student in the Department of Mathematics at the National University of Singapore, advised by Prof. Vincent Tan and Prof. Caroline Chaux.

Research Focus

My research focuses on the theoretical foundations of large language models and the transformer architecture. In particular, my recent work studies the geometry of transformer embedding spaces and its implications for expressiveness, memorization, and approximation capabilities.

I am always happy to discuss new research ideas, collaborations, and internship opportunities in machine learning research and related areas. Feel free to reach out by email if you would like to connect.
